About GoldenDane Tech

Building trustworthy AI for health and industry

Our Mission

GoldenDane Tech is a Danish artificial intelligence company founded at the Technical University of Denmark (DTU). Our mission is to develop high‑impact, trustworthy AI solutions for hospitals, public health systems and industrial engineering partners. By harnessing secure supercomputing infrastructure, we create custom diagnostic and industrial AI models tailored to each client’s data and workflows while adhering to the EU AI Act, GDPR and other relevant regulations.

What We Do

We specialise in end‑to‑end AI development across a range of domains:

  • Clinical AI – diagnostic imaging, laboratory result interpretation and early‑warning scores for sepsis, deterioration or re‑hospitalisation
  • Industrial AI – predictive maintenance, digital twins, condition monitoring and optimisation for energy, manufacturing and infrastructure
  • Research & innovation – bespoke AI prototypes and feasibility studies in collaboration with academic and industry partners

Every model is built from scratch on anonymised or pseudonymised data supplied by our clients. We do not resell generic products; instead we co‑develop solutions that integrate seamlessly into existing processes.

What Makes Us Different

  • Trusted by design – our quality management system covers risk management, data governance, documentation, cybersecurity and post‑market monitoring to meet the EU AI Act’s high‑risk requirements
  • Supercomputer advantage – we operate from DTU and use its secure, high‑performance computing cluster, which accelerates development and reduces cost
  • Human‑centric approach – we design AI systems that keep human experts in the loop, communicate uncertainty clearly and respect domain workflows
  • Transparent pilot programmes – fixed‑scope pilots deliver a working prototype and evaluation report within months, with no long‑term commitment

Data Privacy & Compliance

We understand the sensitivity of health and industrial data. All data is processed under strict European data‑protection rules. We act as a data processor under a signed Data Processing Agreement (DPA); clients remain the data controller. Our processes incorporate the EU AI Act’s requirements for high‑risk systems—risk‑mitigation, high‑quality data sets and human oversight:contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}—and align with the GDPR and the forthcoming European Health Data Space. Anonymisation, pseudonymisation, access controls and audit logs are enforced on all projects.

Our Values

We are guided by four core principles:

  • Trustworthiness – deliver AI systems that are transparent, explainable and safe
  • Collaboration – work closely with clinicians, engineers and researchers to co‑design solutions
  • Innovation – push the boundaries of AI while respecting regulatory and ethical frameworks
  • Impact – focus on applications that improve patient outcomes, operational efficiency and sustainability
