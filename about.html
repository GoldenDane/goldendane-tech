About GoldenDane Tech

Applied AI projects designed for safety, calrity and control

Our Mission

GoldenDane Tech is a Danish AI company focused on building practical, trustworthy AI systems for organisations operating in complex, high-stakes environments.

We work with teams that rely on expert judgment, manual review, or inspection-heavy workflows, helping them apply AI in a controlled, auditable, and human-centred way.

What We Do

We design and deliver narrowly scoped AI projects that support human decision-making rather than replace it.

  • AI-assisted inspection and review systems
  • Risk, anomaly, and prioritisation models
  • Predictive analytics for operational and engineering contexts
  • Custom evaluation and modelling tools built on client data

Each project is co-developed with domain experts and tailored to existing workflows, using data provided by the partner organisation.

How We Work

  • Focused pilot projects – engagements start with a fixed-scope pilot designed to validate value early and safely.
  • Human-in-the-loop design – systems are built to support expert judgment, with clear interfaces and oversight.
  • No off-the-shelf products – every solution is developed specifically for the partner’s data and use case.
  • Clear ownership – partners retain control over their data and resulting models.

Data, Security, and Compliance

We take data protection and system reliability seriously, particularly when working with sensitive or business-critical information.

Projects are developed using anonymised or pseudonymised data wherever possible, under clear contractual agreements. Our approach emphasises auditability, documentation, and appropriate human oversight.

Where relevant, work can be carried out on secure, GDPR-compliant infrastructure, and all solutions are designed to align with applicable European regulatory requirements.

Our Values

  • Trust – build systems that are understandable, reliable, and safe
  • Collaboration – work closely with domain experts at every stage
  • Pragmatism – prioritise usable results over theoretical capability
  • Impact – focus on problems that matter operationally
